###### 2026
#### By Elena Valverde Arcos

Does the news actually find you, or is it just an illusion? As nearly half of young Spaniards rely on social media for news [(European Parliament, 2025)](https://data.europa.eu/doi/10.2861/5146566), many develop a **“News-Finds-Me” perception**—the belief that one’s social and digital presence will keep them informed without active seeking [(Gil de Zúñiga et al., 2017)](https://doi.org/10.1111/jcc4.12185). Focusing on Spanish adults aged 18–29, this study explores an **"illusion of exposure"** by **comparing what users report consuming in a survey with what they actually consume in donated TikTok Viewing Histories**. Because TikTok’s algorithm rarely prioritizes news [(Hagar & Diakopoulos, 2025)](https://doi.org/10.1177/14614448231192964), this research audits news videos for factual building blocks—the Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How [Graber’s (1994)](https://doi.org/10.1177/0957926594005004004). Ultimately, it reveals whether passive consumption creates a truly informed public or just the feeling of one.
